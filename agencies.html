Edmonton Agency Directory

Real organizations supporting unhoused people in Edmonton. Use this as a referral guide.

All agencies listed are real Edmonton organizations. Website links open in a new tab. For immediate referrals, call 211 (Alberta's social services helpline, available 24/7).

Hope Mission Edmonton
Shelter Food Health Outreach
One of Edmonton's largest social service organizations, providing emergency shelter for men, women, and families, a soup kitchen, medical and dental clinic, addiction recovery programs (including the Bridge Recovery Community), and extensive outreach services. Open 24/7 for emergency intake.
780-422-2018 hopemission.com
Boyle Street Community Services
Outreach Health Housing Indigenous Services
A comprehensive downtown drop-in serving Edmonton's most vulnerable residents since 1971. Offers street outreach, mental health and addictions support, harm reduction, housing assistance, legal clinic access, and culturally safe programming for Indigenous community members. Central to the inner-city service network.
780-424-4106 boylestreet.org
Bissell Centre
Shelter Food Housing Employment
Works to break the cycle of poverty through a continuum of services including emergency shelter, transitional housing, a food bank, employment training, life skills programs, and family support services. The Employment Resource Centre offers job placement, resume workshops, and training programs.
780-423-2285 bissellcentre.org
Mustard Seed Edmonton
Shelter Food Outreach
Provides emergency shelter, a food bank, hot meals, clothing, and life skills support to people experiencing homelessness and poverty. Operates on a Christian service ethic with a welcoming environment for all. Also runs street outreach connecting people to community resources.
780-424-7946 mustardseededmonton.ca
Homeward Trust Edmonton
Housing System Planning
The system-planning body responsible for coordinating Edmonton's response to homelessness. Manages Housing First funding, maintains the Coordinated Access system (the formal housing waitlist), and funds a network of housing providers across the city. Not a direct service provider, but the entry point for housing placement.
780-496-2130 homewardtrust.ca
WIN House Edmonton
Shelter Women & Children Housing Support
Edmonton's largest emergency shelter for women and children fleeing domestic violence. Provides confidential emergency accommodation, crisis counselling, safety planning, children's programming, and transitional housing support. Intake is 24/7 and location is not publicly disclosed for safety reasons.
780-479-0058 (24hr) winhouse.org
Jasper Place Wellness Centre
Health Outreach
A community health centre offering primary care, mental health and addictions services, dental care, harm reduction, and social support to people who face barriers accessing mainstream healthcare. Serves predominantly low-income and unhoused residents in west Edmonton. No appointment necessary for walk-in care.
780-757-0981 jpwellness.ca
Edmonton John Howard Society
Housing Employment Reintegration
Supports people involved in or leaving the criminal justice system with reintegration services including transitional housing, employment counselling, life skills, and community supervision support. Addresses the overlap between incarceration and homelessness, particularly for people released without housing or ID.
780-428-7590 edmontonjhs.ab.ca
Catholic Social Services Edmonton
Food Housing Counselling Newcomers
One of Alberta's largest social service agencies, offering a wide range of programs including emergency food assistance, counselling, housing support, newcomer settlement services, family violence intervention, and programs for seniors and people with disabilities. Serves people of all faiths and backgrounds.
catholicsocialservices.ab.ca
Boyle McCauley Health Centre
Health Inner City
A community health centre delivering primary care, mental health services, nursing, dental care, and social support to inner-city residents since 1980. Operates on a sliding-scale fee model and serves patients who are unhoused, precariously housed, or otherwise unable to access mainstream health care. Walk-ins accepted.
bmhc.ca
Service Alberta — ID Document Assistance
ID Services Government
The provincial government office responsible for issuing Alberta ID cards, driver's licences, and birth certificates. Fee waivers are available for people experiencing homelessness. Many shelters can help clients navigate the application process. Note: government-issued ID requires proof of identity, creating a barrier for people who have lost all documents.
alberta.ca/get-id-documents
211 Alberta
Referral Line Information
Alberta's free, 24/7 helpline and online directory for health and human services. Connects callers to shelters, food banks, mental health support, housing resources, legal aid, and hundreds of other services. Available by phone (dial 211), text, or online. Multilingual support available. The best first call for any service navigation need.
Dial or text 2-1-1 ab.211.ca
